I am a Ph.D. student at the University of California Irvine, studying applied cryptography, security, and privacy under Dr. Gene Tsudik and Dr. Stanislaw Jarecki. I graduated from the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT) with a dual BS degree in Computer Science and Software Engineering and an accelerated MS in Computer Science. I also earned minors and concentrations in Mathematics and Writing Studies.

My research interests are very diverse, but the majority of my work is rooted in applied cryptography, computer and network security, and heterogeneous computing. Fortunately, I have had many opportunities to pursue research projects in these areas, which have resulted in several publications, presentations, and unpublished technical reports. As a student and young researcher I try to be involved in projects that span several disciplines to exercise my skills in multiple fields. I also actively pursue teaching positions to share my passion for science and technology with younger students. I also try to actively keep myself in a security-oriented mindset like my brother, Robert Wood, a senior security consultant at Cigital specializing in application and network penetration testing, web and enterprise application source code review, and all around security issues. He's basically my connection back to the real world of security!

In my spare time I also enjoy actively working on my own side projects that are typically unrelated to my main areas of research. These are usually an excuse to learn a new programming language, library, framework, or other technology. The remainder of whatever free time I have left is devoted to music and exercise. My main musical interests are hard rock and heavy metal (i.e., bands such as Avenged Sevenfold, In Flames, Lamb of God, August Burns Red, and Trivium), but I also branch out into acoustic rock, jazz, and classical music. I am the proud owner of a Schecter Hellraiser C-1 (a black model), and am slowly teaching myself the ropes.
