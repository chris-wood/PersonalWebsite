☕ My Daily Grind

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at UCI with a research focus on applied cryptography and network security under Gene Tsudik. I also work as a Secure Transports Engineer at Apple. Previously, I was a core developer of CCNx in the Computer Science Laboratory at PARC (Palo Alto Research Center). I live and work in the Bay Area and study in Irvine.

I graduated with a B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science and B.S. in Software Engineering from RIT in 2013. During my time there I was advised by Dr. Stanislaw Radziszowski. In the past, I worked at Intel, L-3 Communications, and other small software firms. I received the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship (NSF GRFP) in 2014. I am also a member of the IEEE, ACM, IACR, SIAM, Internet Society, and Tau Beta Pi organizations.

💻 Research Software

  1. Distillery: PARC's CCN software stack, forwarder(s), and APIs implemented in C.
  2. testrig: A functional testing and fuzzing application for CCN forwarders.
  3. spud: A user-mode CCN software stack implemented in Go.
  4. iris: A CCN forwarder written in Rust.
  5. ccnx-utils: A suite of tools for CCN research and development. The project includes a packet generator written in Haskell, C library to dissect wire-format messages, C programs to stress test a forwarder, Python program to transform network topologies to forwarder configuration files, and a set of scripts to help aid forwarder experimentation.
