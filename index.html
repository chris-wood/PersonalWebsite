About Me

Christopher A. Wood

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of California Irvine, studying applied cryptography, security, and privacy under Gene Tsudik and Stanislaw Jarecki. Previously, I graduated from the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT) with a dual BS degree in Computer Science and Software Engineering and an accelerated MS in Computer Science.

My research interests are very diverse, including: oblivious and secure multiparty computation, privacy and anonymity intersections with cryptography, symmetric-key cryptographic algorithms, applications, and implementations, information-centric networking security and applications, heterogeneous computing, and computational and extremal graph theory. As of 2014, my research is funded by a multi-year NSG GRFP fellowship.

In my spare time I also enjoy actively working on my own side projects that are typically unrelated to my main areas of research. These are usually an excuse to learn a new programming language, library, framework, or other technology. The remainder of whatever free time I have left is devoted to music and exercise. My main musical interests are hard rock and heavy metal (i.e., bands such as Avenged Sevenfold, In Flames, Lamb of God, August Burns Red, and Trivium), but I also branch out into acoustic rock, jazz, and classical music. I am the proud owner of a Schecter Hellraiser C-1 (a black model), and am slowly teaching myself the ropes.
