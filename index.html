☕ My Daily Grind

I am currently a third-year Ph.D. student at UCI studying applied cryptography and network security under Gene Tsudik. I am also a core developer of CCNx in the Computer Science Laboratory at PARC (Palo Alto Research Center).

In the past, I graduated with a B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science and B.S. in Software Engineering from RIT in 2013, where I was advised by Dr. Stanislaw Radziszowski. In the past, I worked at Intel, L-3 Communications, and other small software firms. I received the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship (NSF GRFP) in 2014, and am also as a member of the IEEE, ACM, IACR, SIAM, Internet Society, and Tau Beta Pi organizations.

📓 Research Interests

Content-centric networking architectures and protocols, computer and network security and privacy, cryptographic algorithms and engineering, software engineering, and heterogeneous computing.

💻 Active Projects

  • CCNx: heavy research and development for the CCNx 1.0 software stack
  • ccn-lite: code contributions
  • COOL (COntent Oriented Lisp): an experimental language built on top of CCN
  • iris: a prototypical CCN forwarder written in Rust
  • CBC: a portable and versatile content-based cryptography library for CCN applications written in C
